I hope to refine my full-stack development skills, particularly in areas like front-end responsiveness and back-end efficiency. Once I leave Dev Academy, I’d like to work in roles that challenge my creativity and technical problem-solving. 3. Strengths and Limitations for Bootcamp

  • Limitations: I still lack confidence in front-end development, particularly in building highly polished UIs.

One major non-technical challenge will be balancing the workload with my personal life, as I’m married with two kids. Staying organized and managing time effectively during an intensive program like Bootcamp could also be challenging.

5. Non-Technical Skills to Develop

I’d like to develop stronger communication skills, particularly in explaining technical concepts to non-technical team members. Improving my leadership and teamwork skills in group projects is another priority, as well as enhancing my ability to receive and give constructive feedback.

6. Expectations from the Bootcamp Team

I expect the Bootcamp team to provide clear guidance, timely feedback, and constructive support when I struggle. I appreciate a balance between independence and assistance, with a focus on problem-solving techniques that can help me develop autonomy.

7. Expectations of Myself on Bootcamp

I expect myself to stay committed to the learning process, engage actively in group work, and seek help when needed. I will push myself to step out of my comfort zone, especially in areas I find challenging, such as front-end work or presenting my ideas to the group.

8. Managing Workload and Seeking Help

To manage my workload, I plan to allocate specific blocks of time each day for focused work and set clear, achievable goals. I will regularly review my progress and seek help early when I hit obstacles, using both peers and instructors as resources. Additionally, I will stay organized using project management tools like Trello or GitHub Issues.
